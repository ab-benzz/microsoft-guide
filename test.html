When checking the electricity consumption of a refrigerator, this can be measured in kilowatt-hour (kWh) used per day. When the kWh is lower, this will consume less electricity, and more cost savings. The checking of the kWh and electricity consumption of a refrigerator can be found on the product’s energy guide label.

A refrigerator typically lasts 10–15 years, depending on usage, brand, and maintenance.

  • Clean condenser coils twice a year.
  • Keep refrigerator door seals clean.
  • Don’t overload shelves for proper airflow.
  • Regularly defrost (manual defrost refrigerators).

Standard warranties may vary per brand, and these generally cover 11-year warranty on parts & labor, with 5–10 years on the compressor.

Consider the layout of your kitchen and measure the space where you intend to place the refrigerator.

  • Measure the width, height, and depth of your chosen refrigerator.
  • Measure door opening space (add 4 inches to the width).
  • Measure air circulation space (add 2–4 inches to height and back) – This ensures that the refrigerator has enough room to circulate air and prevent overheating.
  • Make sure that there are no heat-emitting appliances nearby to maximize the refrigerator’s cooling performance.

During delivery, the refrigerator’s fluids are shaken around, which requires the refrigerator extra time to settle down its fluids before turning on.

  • Wait at least three to four hours before plugging in.
  • Plug the refrigerator on empty load, set to max temperature for two hours.
  • Place your items inside the refrigerator, set to max temperature for two more hours.
  • Set the thermostat to your desired temperature.
